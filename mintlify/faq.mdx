## General

**What is TrueCurrent?**

TrueCurrent is a non-custodial perpetuals exchange on Injective that uses a Request for Quote (RFQ) system. Instead of trading against an automated market maker or a passive order book, you receive live, competitive quotes from professional market makers and execute at the locked-in price with no slippage.

**Is TrueCurrent non-custodial?**

Yes. Your funds stay in your Injective subaccount at all times. TrueCurrent's smart contract only accesses your funds during settlement, via narrow `authz` permissions that you control and can revoke at any time. No TrueCurrent team member or infrastructure can access your wallet.

**What chain is TrueCurrent on?**

Injective. All settlement happens on the Injective blockchain, which provides sub-second finality. Trading on TrueCurrent itself costs you no gas – TrueCurrent pays your Injective transaction fees on your behalf.

**Do I need to pay gas fees?**

No. TrueCurrent covers your Injective transaction costs. You pay no gas for any action taken through TrueCurrent – opening positions, closing them, managing margin, all of it.

## Trading

**What is an RFQ?**

RFQ stands for Request for Quote. When you want to trade, TrueCurrent asks professional market makers to compete for your order in real time. You receive firm, signed quotes and can accept the best one. Unlike AMMs, the price is fixed at quote time and doesn't change during settlement. See [RFQ explained](/overview/rfq-explained) for a full explanation.

**What happens if no market maker responds to my request?**

If no market maker quotes within the response window, your order fails. A future version of TrueCurrent may fall back to the onchain order book in this case, but that feature is not yet enabled – today, you'll need to resubmit.

**What is the worst price setting?**

Worst price is the maximum (for longs) or minimum (for shorts) price you're willing to accept. It's like a slippage tolerance. The TrueCurrent smart contract enforces this onchain – your trade will not execute at a price worse than your worst price.

**Can I get liquidated?**

Yes. Perpetual positions use margin and can be liquidated if the mark price moves enough against your position. Your liquidation price is displayed in the Positions panel. Monitor it, and add margin or reduce your position size if it's getting close. See [Perpetual markets](/trading/perpetual-markets) for details.

**What leverage is available?**

Up to 50x on most markets and 100x on FX markets. Higher leverage means a closer liquidation price. Start with lower leverage while you're getting familiar with the platform.

## Market makers

**How do I become a market maker?**

Apply for whitelist access by contacting the TrueCurrent team. You'll need to complete a testnet integration first to demonstrate your system is working correctly. See [Getting whitelisted](/market-makers/getting-whitelisted).

**What programming languages/SDKs are supported?**

The underlying protocol (WebSocket messages + Injective chain interactions) is language-agnostic. Any language that can maintain a WebSocket connection, implement the quote signing specification, and submit Injective transactions can be used. Python and TypeScript are the best-supported today, with reference implementations available.

**Why is my quote not winning trades?**

Your price may be less competitive than other market makers. Review your pricing model and reference data sources. Also verify that your `authz` grants are current and your margin is sufficient – quote wins that fail at settlement still count against your metrics.

**Can I see who other market makers are?**

Market maker addresses are visible onchain (in the `AcceptQuote` transaction history), but the list of whitelisted makers is not publicly published.

**Can the TrueCurrent smart contract be upgraded or changed?**

The smart contract's upgrade policy will be documented upon mainnet launch. Any upgrades to core settlement logic will be announced in advance through official TrueCurrent channels.

**How do I revoke TrueCurrent's access to my wallet?**

Go to your wallet's authz settings and revoke the grants you gave to the TrueCurrent contract address. This immediately stops the contract from acting on your behalf. You can manage grants from a GUI at [do.injective.network](https://do.injective.network), or see [Authorization model](/technical/authz-model) for the details.
